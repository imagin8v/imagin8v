Making luck

In 1912, after he became the first explorer to reach the South Pole, Roald Amundsen wrote : "Victory awaits him who has everything in order - luck, people call it." No one pretended that misfortune didn't exist, but prior generations believed in making their own luck by working hard.

Indefinite Optimism

To an indefinite optimist, the future will be better, but he doesn't know how exactly, so he won't make any specific plans. A company is the strangest place of all for an indefinite optimist : why should you expect your own business to succeed without a plan to make it happen?

To a definite optimist, the future will be better than the present if he plans and works to make it better.
