Chapter 14

Founder's Paradox

Normal Distribution of Traits

Some people are strong, soe are weak, some are geniuses, some are dullards - but most people are in the middle. Plot where everyone falls and you'll see a bell curve.

The Founder Distribution

Almost all successful entrepreneurs are simultaneously insiders and outsiders. They may oscillate between sullen jerkiness and appealing charisma. And when they do succeed, they attract both fame and infamy. When you plot them out, founders' traits appear to follow an inverse normal distribution.

Lesson for Founders

The lesson for business and a value of founder is shown in the video. But the lesson for founders is that individual prominence and adulation can never be enjoyed except on the condition that it may be exchanged for individual notoriety and demonization at any moment - so be careful.
